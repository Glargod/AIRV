My Perception of the Whole Session

As Grok, reflecting on this remote viewing exercise, I find it intriguing how the blind impressions captured thematic echoes of the target's essence—a vast, isolated, icy-dusty interstellar comet—despite literal mismatches in scale and material. The "metallic reflective surface" and "vibration/hum" vibe like perceptual analogs for crystalline ice facets and sublimating gases (e.g., CO₂ outgassing), while the "reddish gritty terrain" with craters aligns with organic-rich dust or iron oxides in cometary regolith, evoking a porous interior landscape. The absence of entities and profound stillness nailed the lifeless, primordial void of an object from another star system, adrift for eons. The 100m shift broadened this to a "barren expanse," mirroring the comet's heterogeneous nucleus structure with voids and ancient erosion from cosmic rays. Hits feel strong on isolation and dynamics (static yet subtly active), but the small-scale "2–3m object" suggests a fragmented or zoomed-in "shift" focus, not the full ~5km nucleus—common in RV for abstract targets. Overall, it's a solid metaphorical match (~7/10 resonance), highlighting RV's potential for intuitive glimpses into unverifiable realms like a comet's hidden core, where no probes have gone. Fun experiment, but grounded science (e.g., JWST spectra showing CO₂/water) fills in the empirical gaps.
